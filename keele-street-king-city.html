The Thesis

Federal capital builds it. The fund owns the land.

King City entitled land, a 40% Build Canada Homes grant and a CMHC MLI Select mortgage combine so a 30%-affordable rental finances on its own income — with the fund contributing land, not cash. The same repeatable land-unlock behind 8440 and Oakwood.

Step 01

Contribute land, not cash

The fund puts in the $10M King City land. No construction equity, no build risk — the land is the position.

Step 02

Federal capital fills the gap

A 40% Build Canada Homes grant (no repayment) plus a CMHC MLI Select mortgage fund the build at a financeable 1.78× DSCR.

Step 03

Recover, hold, compound

The fund takes ~$7.5M out at close, keeps the asset, 60% of cash flow and a Year-20 buyout.

The Structure · Build Canada Homes + CMHC

The grant is what makes it finance.

As a conventional rental the deal is thin (1.10× coverage). The 40% Build Canada Homes grant — forgivable, no debt service — collapses the debt the income has to carry, taking coverage to a comfortable 1.78×.

Why King City

Affluent, transit-served — and starved of rental.

King City is among York Region's most affluent communities, on the Barrie GO line, with almost no purpose-built rental supply. That makes rents strong and demand deep — while the affordable component answers a real local need and smooths the path to approval.

GO transit

Minutes from King City station on the Barrie line into downtown Toronto.

Thin supply

Almost no purpose-built rental in the village — strong rents, low vacancy, deep demand.

Approvable

Main-street scale and 30% affordable answer the community and the politics of getting built.
