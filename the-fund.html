The Thesis

Federal capital builds it. The fund owns the land.

A 40% Build Canada Homes grant plus a CMHC MLI Select mortgage are intended to let affordable housing finance on its own income — with the fund contributing land, not cash. The strategy is designed to recover a portion of contributed land basis at financing close while the fund keeps the asset, its share of income and a Year-20 buyout right; the timing and amount of any capital returned are not assured. The same mechanic, repeated across seven Ontario sites.

Capital-light

Grants fund ~95%

Federal grants + senior debt + municipal pieces fund the build — the fund's equity is a small slice.

Land-unlock mechanic

Land basis at financing close

The strategy is designed to recover a portion of contributed land basis at each project's financing close; the timing and amount of any capital returned are not assured.

Impact + structure

Affordable, owned, aligned

Permanently affordable homes and an owned asset base. Under the JV terms the Fund retains a 60% share of project cash flow and any Year-20 buyout proceeds — a structural term, not a yield.
