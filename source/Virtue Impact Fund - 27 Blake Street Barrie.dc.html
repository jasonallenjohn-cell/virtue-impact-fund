The Thesis

Federal capital builds it. The fund owns the land.

Approved Barrie land, a 40% Build Canada Homes grant and a CMHC MLI Select mortgage are modelled to combine so a 30%-affordable rental can finance on its own income, subject to grant and financing approval — with the fund contributing land, not cash. The same land-contribution structure the fund intends to run at 8440, Oakwood and Keele Street — here with the entitlement already approved.

Step 01

Contribute land, not cash

The fund puts in the $3.5M Barrie land. No construction equity, no build risk — the land is the position.

Step 02

Federal capital fills the gap

A 40% Build Canada Homes grant (no repayment) plus a CMHC MLI Select mortgage are modelled to fund the build, subject to approval. Eligibility and sizing are not yet confirmed.

Step 03

Recover land, hold, own

The structure contemplates the fund recovering a portion of its land basis at close while retaining a minority interest. Terms are set out in the Offering Memorandum.

The Structure · Build Canada Homes + CMHC

The grant is what makes it finance.

As a conventional rental the deal is thin. The 40% Build Canada Homes grant — forgivable, no debt service — is modelled to reduce the debt the income has to carry and improve debt-service coverage, if the grant and financing are approved on the modelled terms. The City's development-charge waiver lowers the cost on top.

The Land-Unlock · The Fund's Position

$3.5M of land in. A long-dated owned asset.

The structure is designed so that, because the grant and mortgage fund the build, a portion of the fund's land basis can be released as cash at close, with the fund retaining a minority interest, a cash-flow share and a Year-20 buyout right. Amounts and terms are set out in the Offering Memorandum and are not assured; the investment is speculative and capital is at risk.

Pipeline Fit

One thesis. A repeatable pipeline.

Blake Street applies the same land-contribution structure the fund intends to run across its pipeline: become the landowner, let Build Canada Homes and CMHC finance the build, recover a portion of the basis and hold a long-dated interest. These are proposed transactions; none has yet closed or generated returns, and there is no operating track record. Blake is the one already approved.
