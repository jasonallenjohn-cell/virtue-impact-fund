The Thesis

Federal capital builds it. The fund owns the land.

Approved Barrie land, a 40% Build Canada Homes grant and a CMHC MLI Select mortgage combine so a 30%-affordable rental finances on its own income — with the fund contributing land, not cash. The same repeatable land-unlock behind 8440, Oakwood and Keele Street — with the lowest entitlement risk of the four.

Step 01

Contribute land, not cash

The fund puts in the $3.5M Barrie land. No construction equity, no build risk — the land is the position.

Step 02

Federal capital fills the gap

A 40% Build Canada Homes grant (no repayment) plus a CMHC MLI Select mortgage fund the build at a financeable 1.54× DSCR.

Step 03

Recover, hold, compound

The fund takes ~$2.6M out at close, keeps a 5% stake, 60% of cash flow and a Year-20 buyout.

The Structure · Build Canada Homes + CMHC

The grant is what makes it finance.

As a conventional rental the deal is thin. The 40% Build Canada Homes grant — forgivable, no debt service — collapses the debt the income has to carry, taking coverage to a comfortable 1.54×. The City's development-charge waiver lowers the cost on top.
